Yi-Chia is a PhD student at Civil and Environmental Engineering University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign, where he was co-advised by Professor Ximing Cai and Professor Arindam Banerjee. His research focuses on remote sensing, machine learning, and climate change impact assessment. At UIUC, Yi-Chia works on the NSF project: Urban Flood Open Knowledge Network (UF-OKN), contributing to the application of text mining for flood control and evacuation. Yi-Chia finished his M.S. and B.S. at National Taiwan University, where he worked on using remote sensing satellite images and machine learning coupled with flood simulation to assess the impacts of extreme events caused by climate change with Professor Ching-Pin Tung (NTU) and Professor Akiyuki Kawasaki (University of Tokyo). Before joining UIUC, Yi-Chia jointly worked at Taiwan Centers for Disease Control and National Science and Technology Center for Disaster Reduction, where he led the climate change impacts on health research, studying the changing distribution of dengue fever caused by extreme weather events. Yi-Chia was awarded the grant for studying for one year at The University of Tokyo. During his stay at UTokyo, Yi-Chia visited Myanmar for tidal bore observation and collecting land use data. He wrote a series of blog posts for the field trip. In 2018, Yi-Chia joined PASCO Corporation as a deep learning research intern, where he researched convolutional neural networks and satellite imagery.

Interests: remote sensing, machine learning, flooding, water resources management
